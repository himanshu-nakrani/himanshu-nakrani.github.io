## 2026-04-12 - ARIA requirements for Icon-only Buttons
**Learning:** In this application, simply adding a `title` attribute to icon-only buttons (like `ThemeToggle` and `ThemeSelector`) is insufficient for accessibility. While `title` provides a tooltip on hover, screen readers require an explicit `aria-label` to announce the button's purpose, along with appropriate ARIA states (e.g., `aria-pressed`, `aria-expanded`, `aria-haspopup`) to communicate the component's current mode or interaction capability.
**Action:** When creating or reviewing icon-only interactive elements, always pair `title` (or tooltip) with an explicit `aria-label` and the relevant state attributes.## 2024-05-18 - ARIA requirements for Accordions\n**Learning:** In this application, expanding/collapsing sections (like those on the Experience page) lacked proper ARIA attributes, making them inaccessible to screen readers. An accordion toggle button requires `aria-expanded` (to indicate state) and `aria-controls` (linking to the ID of the collapsible content container).\n**Action:** When creating or reviewing accordion-style interactive elements, always include `aria-expanded` and `aria-controls` on the toggle button, and a corresponding `id` on the content container.
